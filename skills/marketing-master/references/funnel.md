# Funnel

Use this reference for funnel architecture, user movement, and stage-by-stage strategy.

## Goal

Design a coherent path from attention to conversion, retention, and reactivation.

## Minimum Inputs

- Business model
- Offer
- Target audience
- Primary conversion
- Sales cycle length
- Available channels
- Product type: digital, service, physical, education, event, or subscription
- Conversion path: landing, instant form, message, webinar, call, checkout, or store
- Data capture: email, WhatsApp, CRM, or none
- Category maturity: well-known market or market that first needs education

## Funnel Mapping

Define these stages as needed:

- Awareness: cold discovery, category creation, problem awareness, curiosity.
- Interest: active curiosity, first relevance, desire, or participation.
- Consideration: capture, education, authority, objection handling, proof, differentiation.
- Decision: direct response, urgency, demo, checkout, booking, or assisted close.
- Loyalty: onboarding, activation, upsell, repeat purchase.
- Advocacy: referral, proof social, public participation, community effect.
- Reactivation: bring back dormant users, leads, or customers.

## For Each Stage

Specify:

- where the user comes from
- how cold, warm, or hot the user is
- stage intention
- what happens in that stage
- what strategy is used
- audience intent
- key pain or desire
- message angle
- offer, mechanism, or next step
- best channel
- data logic:
  what is captured,
  what audience is built,
  when email or CRM starts,
  and how remarketing is created
- CTA
- success metric
- where the user goes next

## Common Outputs

- lead magnet funnel
- webinar funnel
- booked-call funnel
- ecommerce purchase funnel
- nurture funnel
- retargeting funnel
- category-creation funnel
- event-led funnel
- challenge or activation funnel

## Guardrails

- Do not push BOFU messaging at TOFU traffic.
- Do not use the same CTA for every stage.
- Keep one primary conversion per funnel path.
- Make the handoff between content, ads, landing, and email explicit.
- If the market is immature, spend more strategic effort on category creation and activation before pushing hard capture.
- If there is no landing, prefer message, form, event, or community mechanics instead of forcing traffic logic.
- Distinguish strategy from assets:
  first define the funnel mechanics,
  then list possible assets.
