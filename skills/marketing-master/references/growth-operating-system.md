# Growth Operating System

Use this reference when the request is bigger than one marketing asset. The goal is to connect offer, demand, capture, nurture, sales handoff, retention and measurement into one operating loop.

## Core Question

What movement must happen in the market or pipeline?

- Create demand: make people notice a problem, category, contrast or opportunity.
- Capture demand: meet people already searching, comparing or asking.
- Convert demand: reduce risk, prove value and move to purchase, booking or signup.
- Retain demand: activate, onboard, repeat, upsell or prevent churn.
- Reactivate demand: bring dormant leads/customers back with a relevant trigger.

## Growth Architecture

For any full plan, define:

| Layer | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Business goal | Revenue, qualified leads, booked calls, purchases, retention, referrals or brand demand. |
| Offer | What is being sold/promoted, entry point, promise, proof and risk reversal. |
| Audience | Segment, awareness level, pain/desire, buying role and objections. |
| Lifecycle | Stage names, plain-language criteria and next-stage trigger. |
| Channel roles | What paid, organic, SEO, email, WhatsApp, events and sales each do. |
| Data capture | Fields, source, consent, qualification, tags and remarketing audiences. |
| Automation | Trigger, routing, owner, follow-up, failure alert and reporting. |
| Measurement | Stage KPI, conversion event, lead quality signal, learning cadence. |

## Lifecycle Baseline

Adapt stage names to the business, but keep criteria explicit:

1. Unknown audience: has not interacted.
2. Visitor/viewer: consumed content or landed on a property.
3. Captured lead: gave data, messaged, registered or downloaded.
4. Qualified lead: matches fit + intent rules.
5. Sales opportunity: has active commercial conversation.
6. Customer: purchased or contracted.
7. Activated customer: consumed/onboarded successfully.
8. Promoter/referrer: produces proof, referral or repeat demand.
9. Dormant: stopped responding, using or buying.

Do not let lifecycle labels become decoration. Each stage needs entry criteria, owner, next action and reportable timestamp when possible.

## Channel Role Logic

- Meta Ads: speed, creative testing, demand creation/capture, retargeting and lead/message/purchase acquisition.
- Organic social: authority, narrative repetition, proof, community and low-friction demand creation.
- SEO: durable demand capture and education; connect clusters to conversion paths.
- Email: owned nurture, launches, activation, reactivation and one-action advancement.
- WhatsApp/messages: high-context assisted conversion and fast qualification.
- Landing pages/forms: controlled conversion, tracking, qualification and offer clarity.
- Events/webinars/challenges: timed attention, education, urgency and list building.
- CRM/Sheets/Notion: operational memory, ownership, status and follow-up accountability.

## Measurement Rules

- TOFU: reach quality, hook rate, view depth, qualified engagement.
- MOFU: capture rate, cost per qualified lead, content-assisted movement, objection reduction.
- BOFU: booked calls, checkout, conversion rate, sales acceptance, cost per opportunity.
- Retention: activation, repeat purchase, product usage, referrals, churn risk.
- Reactivation: reply rate, recovered opportunities, repeat purchase, unsubscribes.

Avoid optimizing only for the cheapest lead if sales quality drops.
