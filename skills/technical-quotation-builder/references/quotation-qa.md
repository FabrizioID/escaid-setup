# Quotation QA

Use this before finalizing a technical quotation.

## Hierarchy

- Is the client being offered one proposal, several alternatives, or one proposal with internal activities?
- Are deliverables separated from activities/acapites?
- Are pilots, discounts, and additional items clearly marked?
- Does every table row represent a saleable unit?

## Scope

- Are inclusions specific and non-duplicated?
- Are exclusions explicit where risk exists?
- Are assumptions visible when the price depends on client-provided information?
- Does the wording avoid promising design, validation, or redesign unless included?

## Pricing Logic

- Does the unit match the work?
- Are formulas or rates shown without inventing missing quantities?
- Is internal support kept separate from client-facing simplicity?
- Do pilot discounts show base price and commercial adjustment when useful?

## Consistency

- Opening paragraph, scope section, economic table, payment section, and terms must tell the same story.
- Old alternatives or previous scopes must be removed after scope changes.
- Terminology must stay stable across the document.
