# Hook Taxonomy

Generate 5 hooks per script. Use different hook categories when possible.

Each hook has 3 required layers and 1 optional layer:

- Verbal: exact spoken line.
- Visual: what appears in the first seconds.
- Textual: on-screen overlay or caption.
- Auditory: optional music, sound effect, silence, or impact cue.

## Categories

### 1. Educational Hook

Promise useful knowledge.

Patterns:

- "Lo que nadie te dice sobre X"
- "El error que comete el 80% de X"
- "Como lograr [resultado] en [tiempo]"

Aggressiveness: low to medium.

### 2. Comparison Hook

Use contrast to trigger attention.

Patterns:

- "X vs Y"
- "Antes hacia X, ahora hago Y"
- "Por que X funciona mejor que Y"

Aggressiveness: medium.

### 3. Myth Busting Hook

Break an established belief.

Patterns:

- "El mito de X"
- "Te mintieron sobre X"
- "Esto que todos hacen esta mal"

Aggressiveness: medium to high.

### 4. Storytelling Hook

Open a narrative loop.

Patterns:

- "El dia que X cambio todo"
- "Nadie me creyo cuando dije X"
- "Cometi el error mas caro de mi vida"

Aggressiveness: low to medium.

### 5. Authority Hook

Position credibility before the message.

Patterns:

- "Despues de X anos haciendo Y"
- "He trabajado con [numero] de [audiencia]"
- "Los datos que no quieren que veas"

Aggressiveness: low.

### 6. Day In The Life Hook

Create identification through routine or behind-the-scenes access.

Patterns:

- "Un dia normal en X"
- "Lo que hace [perfil] cada manana"
- "La rutina que nadie muestra"

Aggressiveness: low.

### 7. Random / Disruptive Hook

Break scroll pattern with something unexpected, abrupt, or apparently contradictory.

Patterns:

- abrupt start without context
- unexpected question
- radical claim
- apparent contradiction

Aggressiveness: high.

## Hook Quality Rules

A strong hook should:

- create immediate tension, curiosity, contrast, or recognition
- be specific enough to avoid sounding generic
- match the awareness level of the audience
- be easy to say out loud
- fit inside the first 1-3 seconds
- imply a reason to keep watching

Do not write generic hooks that could apply to any topic.

## Hook Resolution Rules (Critical)

Never resolve the hook. The hook opens a question the viewer can only close by watching.

- Never name the solution in the hook. Use "esto", "esta herramienta", "lo que descubrí", "usando esto".
- If the viewer can read the hook and leave satisfied, rewrite it.
- The closer "usando esto" / "con esto" / "gracias a esto" is the most efficient implicit CTA — it points to a specific unnamed solution.
- A hook that answers the how, the what, or the with-what is a dead hook.

## Two-Text Format Rules

When using two-text format (large text + small complement):

- The small text must deepen curiosity, not resolve it. It opens a second question on top of the first.
- The first line can intentionally lower the viewer's guard so the second line hits harder.
  Example: "Coordinar BIM en proyectos hospitalarios no es más difícil que edificaciones." → "...no es más difícil. Es diferente."
- Use "estos" over "los" when pointing to specific unnamed elements — creates stronger sense of concrete unknown.
  Example: "...y sigo viendo cometer estos mismos errores" > "...los mismos errores"

## Schema-Breaker Rules

- A schema-breaker only works if there is a real technical truth the video can reveal.
- Creating tension with claims that do not hold up technically is humo — it loses the viewer who knows and misleads the one who does not.
- Before writing a schema-breaker, internally answer: "Is this defensible? Can the video resolve this claim with something real?"
- The emotion must come from within the viewer's world (their daily tools, tasks, behaviors), not from consequences outside their domain.

## Setup Pivot Rule

At the end of the setup section, a verbal pivot creates anticipation before the body drops.

Strong pivots: "Peeero...", "Hasta que...", "Excepto por uno...", "Y entonces..."

The pivot signals to the viewer that the assumption they just accepted is about to break. They cannot leave.

## Extra Pressure From Formula 100K

When hooks feel too polite, use a stronger style:

- Negative warning: "No hagas X si..."
- Prohibition: "Nunca uses X para..."
- Information gap: "Seguro no sabias esto sobre..."
- Challenge: "El 90% falla aqui..."
- Rule of 3: "3 errores / 3 capas / 3 senales..."
- Rule of 2: "X vs Y" with a clear consequence.
- Controversial claim: "X esta sobrevalorado si..."
- Limiting belief: "Si crees X, te equivocas."
- Statistic hook: a number that creates proof or tension.
- Psychology hook: name the bias, fear, or false assumption.
- Filtering hook: name the exact audience in the first line.
- Impact summary: show the result or risk first.

For every hook, combine:

```text
clarity + context + curiosity
```

The first 3 seconds must include:

- verbal hook
- visual hook
- textual hook, 5-7 words max

If a hook does not imply a point-A to point-B reward, rewrite it.
